Development of Supercapacitor Using Carbon Aerogel

Final Year B.Tech Project (S7–S8) — Dept. of Electrical & Electronics Engineering, RIT Kottayam

Abstract

This project develops a high-performance supercapacitor using carbon aerogel synthesized from lignin. Carbon aerogels provide very high surface area, lightweight structure and good electronic conductivity. The work covers synthesis (PVA–lignin method), carbonization, electrode fabrication and electrochemical evaluation.

Motive

  • Conventional supercapacitors have limited energy density and can be costly.
  • Many electrode materials rely on non-renewable feedstocks; lignin is abundant and sustainable.
  • Carbon aerogels can deliver scalable, eco-friendly electrodes with high surface area.

Objectives

  • Develop a cost-effective supercapacitor using lignin-derived carbon aerogel.
  • Optimize porous structure to improve energy density & conductivity.
  • Demonstrate long cycle life and mechanical stability.
  • Benchmark against conventional activated carbon electrodes.

Methodology

Preparation of Carbon Aerogel from Lignin (PVA–Lignin method)

StageProcedure
Solution Preparation5% PVA solution blended with lignin (3:1 ratio). Mix thoroughly to form homogeneous precursor sol.
Deep FreezingSolidification at −20 °C for 12–48 hours to produce cryogel structure.
Freeze DryingSublimation of water under vacuum to obtain porous aerogel network.
Vacuum DryingRemoval of residual moisture and solvents at moderate temperature under vacuum.
CarbonizationHeat treatment up to 1000 °C in inert atmosphere to form conductive carbon aerogel.
Electrode FabricationMix aerogel powder with conductive additive and binder, coat on current collector and dry.
Device AssemblyAssemble electrodes with separator and electrolyte into test cells and package as supercapacitor.
CharacterizationMeasure BET surface area, porosity, cyclic voltammetry, galvanostatic charge/discharge, EIS and cycle life.

Results & Discussion (Summary)

Lignin-derived carbon aerogels show enhanced specific capacitance and improved ion transport due to interconnected porous architecture. Optimised carbonization and electrode processing yield lower ESR and stable cycling behavior (>5000 cycles with <10% loss).
