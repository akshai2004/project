Development of Supercapacitor Using Carbon Aerogel

Final Year B.Tech Project (S7–S8) — Dept. of Electrical & Electronics Engineering, RIT Kottayam

Abstract

A supercapacitor is developed using carbon aerogel synthesized from lignin. Carbon aerogels offer high surface area, low weight, and good conductivity. The project involves aerogel synthesis, characterization, and device fabrication. The aim is to create a sustainable, high-performance energy storage device suitable for applications like EVs, portable electronics, grid storage, and IoT systems.

Motive

  • Conventional supercapacitors face limitations in energy density and cost.
  • Existing electrode materials rely on non-renewable and expensive resources.
  • Carbon aerogels offer eco-friendly, sustainable, and scalable solutions.

Objectives

  • Develop a cost-effective supercapacitor using carbon aerogel.
  • Improve energy density and conductivity with optimized porous structures.
  • Ensure long cycle life and mechanical stability.
  • Compare with conventional supercapacitors for performance validation.

Methodology

Preparation of Carbon Aerogel from Lignin (PVA–Lignin method):

  • Solution Preparation — 5% PVA solution blended with lignin (3:1 ratio).
  • Deep Freezing — solidification at –20 °C for 12–48 hours.
  • Freeze Drying — sublimation of water under vacuum.
  • Vacuum Drying — removal of residual moisture.
  • Carbonization — heating up to 1000 °C to obtain carbon aerogel.
